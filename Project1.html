Data analysis, visualization & reporting

At UNHCR, I led a significant survey analysis project, tackling urgent issues faced by refugees in Jordan. My role involved diving into complex datasets, extracting meaningful insights using statistical techniques, and transforming findings into impactful visuals. With R, I analyzed the data, while Adobe Illustrator and InDesign brought the results to life. Below you can find the four reports that were produced for this project.

In addition, I played a key part in developing another study titled "Exacerbating Vulnerabilities: Where do Global Shocks Leave Refugees in Jordan?" where we explored the impact of external shocks like COVID-19 and the Russian invasion of Ukraine on the socio-economic situations of people in Jordan. The study revealed differential recovery patterns for refugees in camps versus those outside, highlighting the unique challenges they face. For this study, I conducted extensive research and data analysis specifically for the "Russian invasion of Ukraine" section. To bring the report to life, I created visuals using R and Adobe Illustrator that were featured throughout all sections. Lastly, I compiled and designed the final report using Adobe InDesign to ensure a polished and professional presentation.
